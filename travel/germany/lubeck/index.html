Where to Stay in Lübeck – Best Areas & Hotels + Deals

Lübeck is one of northern Germany’s most beautiful historic cities, known for its Old Town island, medieval gates, riverside views and easy access to Travemünde by the Baltic Sea. This guide helps you choose the best area to stay based on sightseeing, transport, food and short trips.

Best areas to stay in Lübeck

For most visitors, the best choice is Lübeck Altstadt. For easier train access, stay near Hauptbahnhof. For a beach-style side trip, Travemünde is the better option.

Best overall

Lübeck Altstadt / Old Town

Best for first-time visitors, sightseeing, restaurants and walking around the historic center. This is the most atmospheric area and keeps you close to the city’s main landmarks.

Transport choice

Hauptbahnhof area

Good for travelers arriving by train or planning day trips. It is practical, usually easier for luggage, and still close enough to reach the Old Town on foot or by local transport.

Landmark stay

Holstentor area

A scenic choice near Lübeck’s famous city gate. Great for photos, short walks into the Old Town and staying close to both the historic center and station side.

Riverside feel

Trave River area

Best for visitors who want quieter views, riverside walks and a more relaxed stay while still being close to the central attractions.

Beach side trip

Travemünde

Best if you want a Baltic Sea feel, beach walks and a more resort-style stay. It works well as a side trip from Lübeck or as a slower coastal base.

Budget angle

Outside the Old Town

Staying just outside the center can offer better value, especially during weekends or busy travel dates. Check transport distance before booking.

Quick recommendation

If it is your first time in Lübeck, choose Altstadt / Old Town. It gives you the easiest access to the historic streets, cafés, museums, churches, shops and evening walks.

  • Best for sightseeing: Lübeck Altstadt
  • Best for train access: Hauptbahnhof area
  • Best for photos: Holstentor and Old Town edge
  • Best for relaxed views: Trave River area
  • Best for beach trip: Travemünde

Booking tip

For short stays, do not choose a hotel too far from the Old Town unless the price difference is worth it. Lübeck is best enjoyed on foot, especially around Altstadt and the river.
  • Check walking distance to Altstadt or Hauptbahnhof.
  • Compare hotel prices before weekends and holidays.
  • Travemünde is better for beach mood than city sightseeing.

How many days to stay in Lübeck?

A one-night stay is enough for a quick Old Town visit, but two nights is better if you want a relaxed pace, riverside walks, museums and a possible trip to Travemünde.

  • 1 night: Old Town walk, Holstentor, dinner and quick sightseeing.
  • 2 nights: Better pace, more cafés, museums and river views.
  • 3 nights: Add Travemünde or a slower northern Germany itinerary.

FAQs

Quick answers before choosing your Lübeck hotel area.

What is the best area to stay in Lübeck for first-time visitors?

Lübeck Altstadt is the best overall area for first-time visitors because it puts you close to the main historic streets, landmarks, restaurants and evening walks.

Is Lübeck Old Town walkable?

Yes. The Old Town is compact enough for most visitors to explore on foot, especially if your hotel is inside or near the Altstadt.

Should I stay near Lübeck Hauptbahnhof?

Stay near Hauptbahnhof if you are arriving by train, carrying luggage or planning day trips. For atmosphere, Altstadt is usually better.

Is Travemünde better than Lübeck city center?

Travemünde is better for beach and Baltic Sea atmosphere. Lübeck city center is better for Old Town sightseeing, restaurants and historic attractions.
