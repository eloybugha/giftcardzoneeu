Why visit Vietnam?

Historic cities, tropical beaches and unforgettable food

Vietnam offers energetic cities, old quarters, mountain scenery, tropical islands, UNESCO-listed sites, river landscapes and one of Southeast Asia’s most popular food cultures.

  • Explore Hanoi’s Old Quarter, lakes, temples and cafés.
  • Experience Ho Chi Minh City’s markets and urban energy.
  • Relax on beaches in Da Nang, Nha Trang or Phu Quoc.
  • Visit Ha Long Bay, Hoi An and scenic northern regions.
  • Build a north-to-south multi-city itinerary.

Useful travel details

Things to consider before booking

Major airports

Common gateways include Hanoi Noi Bai, Ho Chi Minh City Tan Son Nhat and Da Nang International Airport.

Domestic travel

Domestic flights can connect distant regions, while trains and buses may suit slower multi-city journeys.

Entry requirements

Visa and passport requirements depend on nationality and may change. Verify current official rules before departure.

Weather differences

Vietnam’s north, center and south can experience different seasonal weather conditions throughout the year.

Hotel location

Compare neighborhood, beach access, local transportation and airport distance before choosing accommodation.

MyTrip Vietnam booking FAQ

Can I compare flights and hotels in Vietnam with MyTrip?

Yes. MyTrip lets travelers search available flights and accommodation for destinations across Vietnam. Prices and availability depend on the route and selected dates.

Which Vietnam cities can I search?

Travelers can search Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, Da Nang, Hue, Nha Trang, Phu Quoc and other destinations. Ha Long Bay and Hoi An may be reached through nearby transportation gateways.

Which airport should I choose for Vietnam?

The best airport depends on your itinerary. Hanoi is convenient for northern Vietnam, Ho Chi Minh City for the south and Da Nang for central Vietnam and Hoi An.

Can I plan a multi-city Vietnam trip?

Yes. Many travelers combine Hanoi, Ha Long Bay, Da Nang, Hoi An and Ho Chi Minh City. Compare domestic flights, trains and ground transportation when planning the route.
